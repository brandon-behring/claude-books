<KeyIdea>
Delegate vs collaborate is the wrong frame — both are about *who does the work*. **Interview is the third frame**, and it is about *who does the thinking*. The cure for sycophantic, pattern-matched answers is to make Claude surface reasoning, uncertainty, and alternatives before you commit.
</KeyIdea>

## The four patterns

| Pattern | When to reach for it | What it produces |
|---|---|---|
| **Hypothesis debugging** | A symptom under-determines its cause (failing test, drifting metric, intermittent error) | 2-4 ranked hypotheses, each with the cheapest evidence that would confirm or rule it out |
| **Pre-mortem** | Before an irreversible decision (vendor choice, schema change, deprecation) | 3+ named, specific failure modes with detection signals and cheapest mitigations |
| **Feynman** | You suspect your understanding of a topic is thinner than it feels | A list of the vague sentences, undefended claims, and clarifying questions in your own explanation |
| **Interview** | At the start of any non-trivial task | 5-7 decision-shaping questions Claude wants answered before producing anything |

All four share one shape: **make Claude do work that is harder to fake than producing a fluent answer.**

## Anti-sycophancy red flags

<MarginNote variant="warning" label="Warning">
  Sycophancy is highest at session-start when Claude has the least context.
</MarginNote>

- "You're absolutely right" before any file is read or test is run
- A fix proposed without a single `Read` tool call above it
- Exactly one diagnosis with no alternatives mentioned

The mechanical fix: take the load-bearing claim out of your prompt, and ask Claude to reach the claim from the evidence.

## ADRs in one sentence

**Architecture Decision Records** are the durable artifact the four patterns produce — three sections (Context / Decision / Consequences) that capture *why* a decision was made so a future you or a future Claude session does not start from zero.
